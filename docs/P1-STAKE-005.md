### 1.1 Purpose, Scope, and Context

**Purpose:** This document identifies, classifies, and defines engagement strategies for all stakeholders involved in the Quantum Nexus Forge / Sentinel Forge Cognitive AI Orchestration Platform. It serves as the authoritative reference for understanding who holds interest or influence over the project, how they should be communicated with, and how their engagement should be managed throughout the project lifecycle.

**Scope:** This analysis covers all internal team members, AI collaborators, target end-user populations, advisory groups, and community contributors associated with the platform from Phase 1 (Initiation) through anticipated production release. It does not cover regulatory bodies or investors, as the project is currently self-directed and open-source.

**Context:** Sentinel Forge is a cognitive AI orchestration platform designed specifically for neurodivergent adults — individuals with ADHD, dyslexia, dyscalculia, and autism spectrum conditions. The project is architected by Shannon Brian Kelly, a 55-year-old Certified Nursing Assistant who lives with ADHD, dyslexia, dysgraphia, and monitors for autism. The development model uses a unique Driver/Navigator pair-programming paradigm where Claude AI (Anthropic) serves as the engineering Driver handling heavy coding and implementation, and Grok (xAI) serves as the Navigator providing vision, accessibility guidance, SDLC oversight, and user orchestration. This lived-experience foundation directly shapes stakeholder priorities and communication strategies.

---

### 1.2 Assumptions and Constraints

**Assumptions:**

- All AI collaborators (Claude AI, Grok) remain available and capable throughout the project lifecycle.
- Neurodivergent end users will be reachable for feedback through online communities, accessibility forums, and healthcare networks.
- The project will remain open-source and community-driven for the foreseeable future.
- Shannon Kelly retains full decision-making authority as Product Owner and Architect.
- Healthcare worker feedback channels can be established through Shannon's professional CNA network.

**Constraints:**

- Shannon Kelly is the sole human team member; all other core contributors are AI systems with inherent limitations on autonomy and continuity.
- Budget is limited to personal resources; no external funding is currently secured.
- Communication with end users depends on voluntary participation; no formal user research contracts exist.
- AI collaborator availability is subject to platform policies, rate limits, and service continuity from Anthropic and xAI respectively.
- Shannon's neurodivergent conditions (ADHD, dyslexia, dysgraphia) require all documentation and communication to follow accessible formatting standards.

| Advocate | Actively promotes and champions the project and its goals |
| Supportive | Generally positive and willing to contribute when asked |
| Neutral | Neither actively supports nor opposes; engaged passively |

**Quadrant Strategies:**

- **Manage Closely (High Influence, High Interest):** Shannon Kelly and Claude AI are the core decision-making and execution pair. Every major architectural, design, and implementation decision flows through them. Daily collaboration sessions and structured handoffs are essential.
- **Keep Satisfied (High Influence, Low Interest):** No stakeholders currently occupy this quadrant. If external funders or institutional partners join the project, they would likely land here.
- **Keep Informed (Low Influence, High Interest):** Grok, end users, healthcare workers, and accessibility advocates care deeply about outcomes but do not directly control project direction. Regular updates, feedback opportunities, and transparent progress reporting keep these stakeholders engaged and valued.
- **Monitor (Low Influence, Low Interest):** The open source community observes from a distance. Maintaining clean documentation, clear licensing, and responsive issue management is sufficient.
